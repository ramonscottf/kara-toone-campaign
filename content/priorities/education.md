    For over a decade, Kara has worked directly in Davis County education as an executive at the Davis County Education Foundation. She knows our schools intimately. She knows our 72,000+ students. She knows our 90+ schools. And she knows our 5,000+ educators—the people who show up every day to shape our children's futures.

    Education is the most important function of local government. Everything else flows from it. Children who receive a great education are healthier, earn more, commit fewer crimes, and build stronger families and communities. Great schools make communities thrive.

    Yet our schools are underfunded. Teachers struggle with stagnant salaries and increasing class sizes. Schools lack modern facilities and technology. Mental health services are inadequate. Student achievement gaps persist.

    Kara believes that investing in education is the best investment any county can make. She's committed to fully funding our schools and supporting the teachers and students who make them great.

sections:
  - eyebrow: "Kara's Foundation"
    title: "Davis County Education Foundation Leadership"
    content: |
      Kara's role at the Davis County Education Foundation has given her deep insight into our schools and the challenges they face. The Foundation's mission is to support students, teachers, and schools through grants, programs, and partnerships.

      Through this work, Kara has:
      - Built relationships with every school administrator in Davis County
      - Funded classroom grants and student programs across the district
      - Supported teacher professional development and recognition
      - Worked with schools on equity and access for all students
      - Coordinated partnerships between schools and community organizations
      - Understood the real budget challenges schools face

      This direct experience means Kara understands education not as an abstract policy issue, but as the real work of helping real teachers teach real students.

  - eyebrow: "Funding and Accountability"
    title: "Adequate School Funding and Financial Transparency"
    content: |
      Schools need adequate funding. Not just to survive, but to thrive. Kara supports:

      - County funding for school priorities identified by educators and schools
      - Transparent budgeting that shows where education dollars go
      - Support for capital facility improvements (buildings, technology, infrastructure)
      - Investment in early childhood programs that prepare children for school
      - Special education funding that meets the needs of all students
      - Programs for English language learners and underserved populations

      The county doesn't directly fund schools (that's a state and federal responsibility), but the county can support school initiatives and capital projects. Kara will ensure that support is robust and accountable.

  - eyebrow: "Students and Families"
    title: "Student Success and Family Support"
    content: |
      Great schools serve whole students and families. Kara supports:

      - Mental health services and counseling for students
      - After-school and summer programs that keep students engaged
      - Family engagement programs that involve parents in education
      - Career and technical education that prepares students for jobs
      - College and vocational planning support
      - Programs addressing food insecurity and basic needs
      - Support for students dealing with trauma and adversity

      Education happens best when schools and families work together, and when students get support beyond just classroom instruction.

  - eyebrow: "Teachers and Staff"
    title: "Supporting Educators"
    content: |
      Teachers are the foundation of great schools. Kara's commitment:

      - Advocate for competitive teacher salaries
      - Support professional development and career advancement
      - Recognize and celebrate teacher excellence
      - Work toward reducing class sizes
      - Support school staff (counselors, nurses, paraprofessionals)
      - Foster school cultures that prioritize teacher wellness
      - Advocate for working conditions that allow teachers to focus on teaching

      Great schools need great teachers. And great teachers need support, respect, and adequate compensation.

  - eyebrow: "Equity and Excellence"
    title: "Closing Achievement Gaps"
    content: |
      All students deserve a quality education, regardless of zip code, family income, or background. Kara believes in:

      - Additional resources for schools serving lower-income students
      - Programs addressing specific achievement gaps
      - Diverse and inclusive curriculum that reflects all students
      - Support for students with disabilities and learning differences
      - Culturally responsive teaching and leadership
      - Recruitment of educators that reflect student diversity
      - Regular monitoring of equity outcomes

      Excellence means not just having great schools—it means having great schools for all students.

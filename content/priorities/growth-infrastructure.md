  content: |
    Davis County is experiencing unprecedented growth. Over the last decade, our population has grown by 25%, and the growth shows no signs of slowing. This is good news—it reflects the appeal of our community and the opportunity it offers families. But without thoughtful planning and strategic infrastructure investment, rapid growth can overwhelm us.

    Today, Davis County faces a $4.2 billion infrastructure gap. Commute times have increased to an average of 34 minutes. Water and utility systems are strained. Roads are congested. Schools are overcrowded. The question isn't whether we'll grow—it's whether we'll grow smartly, with the infrastructure and planning in place to handle that growth.

    Kara believes growth can be managed sustainably, but it requires county leadership that understands both development and community needs.

sections:
  - eyebrow: "Smart Growth"
    title: "Transportation and Roads"
    content: |
      A thriving county needs modern transportation infrastructure. The I-15 expansion, though federal, requires county coordination and planning. But roads are only part of the answer. Kara supports:

      - Coordinated regional transportation planning with neighboring counties
      - Investment in public transit options to reduce single-occupancy vehicle dependence
      - Smart corridor management that maximizes the efficiency of existing roads
      - Pedestrian and bike infrastructure in growing communities
      - Strategic placement of new growth to minimize commute distances

      The goal isn't to stop growth—it's to make sure growth patterns work *with* our infrastructure, not against it.

  - eyebrow: "Essential Services"
    title: "Water, Utilities, and Services"
    content: |
      Growth strains water and utility systems. Davis County needs to be proactive about securing water rights, upgrading utility infrastructure, and planning for future capacity. Kara's approach:

      - Work with Utah Division of Water Resources on long-term water security
      - Coordinate with water districts on infrastructure investment and efficiency
      - Plan utility expansions concurrent with approved development
      - Invest in water conservation and alternative water sources
      - Ensure that utilities can handle growth without compromising service quality

      Ensuring adequate water and utilities isn't glamorous work, but it's fundamental to sustaining growth.

  - eyebrow: "Thoughtful Development"
    title: "Smart Growth and Community Character"
    content: |
      Not all growth is created equal. Some development patterns create walkable communities and preserve open space. Others sprawl, waste resources, and damage quality of life. Kara believes the county should:

      - Work with cities on comprehensive land use planning
      - Encourage higher-density development near transit and services
      - Preserve agricultural land and open space where possible
      - Support mixed-use development that reduces car dependence
      - Ensure that new growth includes adequate schools, parks, and services
      - Require developers to contribute to infrastructure needs they create

      Smart growth means we grow in ways that create better communities, not just bigger ones.

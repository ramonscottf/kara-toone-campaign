    Taxpayers in Davis County work hard for their money. They deserve a government that respects that sacrifice. A government that spends wisely. A government that is transparent about how money is spent. A government that eliminates waste and maximizes value.

    Kara believes in 100% transparency in government spending. She believes county government should operate with zero waste and maximum efficiency. And she believes local communities—not distant bureaucrats—should control resources and make decisions about priorities.

    Fiscal responsibility is foundational to good government. It's how you build taxpayer trust.

sections:
  - eyebrow: "Transparency"
    title: "100% Government Spending Transparency"
    content: |
      Government budgets should be open books. Every dollar of taxpayer money should be trackable. Yet budget documents are often complex and hard to understand. Government meetings happen with little public notice. Spending priorities aren't clearly justified to taxpayers.

      Kara's commitment to transparency includes:
      - Simple, understandable budget documents available online
      - Regular public reporting on how county money is spent
      - Clear justification for all major expenditures
      - Open meetings with public notice and opportunity for comment
      - Whistleblower protections for employees reporting waste or fraud
      - Annual performance reports showing results for every major program
      - Public databases of county contracts and spending

      Transparency doesn't just build trust—it creates accountability and prevents waste.

  - eyebrow: "Efficiency"
    title: "Eliminating Waste and Government Efficiency"
    content: |
      Government should operate like a well-run business. That means:

      - Regular audits to identify inefficiencies and waste
      - Performance-based budgeting that ties spending to results
      - Regular review of programs and spending for continued relevance
      - Competitive bidding for contracts to ensure fair pricing
      - Technology investment that reduces administrative burden
      - Shared services between departments and jurisdictions where possible
      - Zero-based budgeting reviews to challenge assumptions about spending

      Efficiency doesn't mean cutting services—it means getting maximum value from every dollar, so more resources go to the public rather than administration.

  - eyebrow: "Local Control"
    title: "Local Communities Making Local Decisions"
    content: |
      Utah County isn't monolithic. Davis County isn't monolithic. Communities have different needs and values. The people living in a community understand its challenges better than government administrators or distant legislators.

      Kara believes in local control:
      - County government deferring to city governments where appropriate
      - Communities having voice in decisions that affect them
      - Resources following decisions, not decisions following bureaucratic mandates
      - Regional approaches only when communities agree they make sense
      - Protection of local authority from state and federal overreach
      - Community input processes that are genuine and influential

      Government closest to the people is most responsive and accountable to them.

  - eyebrow: "Partnership"
    title: "Government-Community Partnership"
    content: |
      The best solutions come from government, business, nonprofit, and community working together. Kara supports:

      - Public-private partnerships for infrastructure and services
      - County support for nonprofit organizations serving community needs
      - Business-friendly policies that create jobs and tax base
      - Community-based problem-solving rather than top-down mandates
      - Diverse voices in government decision-making
      - Regular engagement with community leaders and organizations
      - Respect for community knowledge and experience

      Government's role is to enable and support community, not to control it.

  - eyebrow: "Unfunded Mandates"
    title: "Pushing Back on Unfunded State and Federal Mandates"
    content: |
      State and federal governments often impose requirements without funding them. Counties bear the cost. Kara believes:

      - State and federal requirements should include funding
      - Counties should have flexibility in how to meet mandates
      - Unfunded mandates should be rejected or challenged
      - Counties should work together to push back on unfunded requirements
      - Local solutions should be preferred to one-size-fits-all mandates
      - Cost-benefit analysis should guide acceptance of new requirements

      Counties shouldn't be forced to choose between unfunded mandates and core services.

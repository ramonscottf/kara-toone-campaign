sections:
  - eyebrow: "Starter Homes"
    title: "Enabling First-Time Homebuyers"
    content: |
      Young families need starter homes. These are modest, affordable homes that allow people to build equity and stability. Kara supports:

      - Zoning changes that allow smaller-lot single-family homes
      - Support for missing-middle housing (duplexes, townhomes, small apartments)
      - Down payment assistance programs for first-time buyers
      - Community land trust models that keep homes affordable permanently
      - Incentives for builders to include starter homes in new developments

      The goal is simple: give young families a way to own their first home and build their future in Utah County.

  - eyebrow: "Developer Partnerships"
    title: "Speeding Permitting and Reducing Development Costs"
    content: |
      Housing costs are driven partly by limited supply and restrictive permitting processes. Development takes years and costs hundreds of thousands in fees. Those costs get passed to homebuyers. Kara will:

      - Streamline permitting while maintaining quality standards
      - Reduce development impact fees where appropriate
      - Coordinate between county and cities on consistent standards
      - Support modular and alternative construction methods
      - Work with developers to create more workforce housing
      - Create incentive programs for housing projects serving lower-income families

      Faster permitting and lower development costs mean more homes and more affordable prices.

  - eyebrow: "Local Control"
    title: "Empowering Local Communities"
    content: |
      Housing policy works best when cities and the county work together. Kara believes in:

      - Strong city-county coordination on housing policy
      - Supporting local control while encouraging housing diversity
      - Sharing best practices across municipalities
      - County support for cities that take on challenging affordable housing projects
      - Regional planning that respects community character while allowing appropriate growth

      Not every city needs the same housing solutions. But all should have county support for creating workforce housing.

  - eyebrow: "Programs & Support"
    title: "First-Time Buyer Assistance"
    content: |
      Market forces alone won't solve the affordability crisis. Government can help with direct support:

      - First-time homebuyer down payment assistance
      - Shared equity models where government retains interest in affordability
      - Property tax relief for affordable rental housing
      - Support for nonprofit developers and land trusts
      - Financing assistance for in-fill development and renovations

      These programs require investment, but they pay for themselves through economic growth and community stability.
